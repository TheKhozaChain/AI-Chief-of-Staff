## Recommendations

### Immediate (this week)

1. **Deduplicate the validation queue** — Consolidate identical-param clusters to single representative. Reduces queue from 27 to ~10–12.
2. **Raise promotion threshold from PF ≥ 1.3 to PF ≥ 1.5** — Strategies at PF 1.30–1.35 rarely survive validation + costs. Fewer promotions = manageable queue.
3. **Add dedup check to screening pipeline** — Compare new strategy params against existing promoted list before promoting. Auto-kill duplicates.
4. **Sync or retire todo.json** — Either update from KANBAN state or remove from pipeline.

### Medium-term (next 2 weeks)

5. **Automate walk-forward validation** — Build `batch_validate.py` that runs walk-forward on all promoted strategies, auto-kills OOS PF < 1.0 or decay > 50%. Converts bottleneck from "4 hours per strategy" to "review a summary table."
6. **Add staleness detection** — If same recommendation appears 3+ consecutive briefs without action, escalate differently.
7. **Diversify LLM sourcing prompts** — Rotate between archetypes to reduce duplicate generation.

### Longer-term

8. **Pipeline dashboard** — One-glance summary table: ideas → screened → promoted → validated → paper trading → live.
9. **Reduce sourcing cadence** — 3 ideas/day is 21/week; validation capacity is ~2–5/week. Either reduce sourcing or accept permanent backlog growth.

## Priority Action: Break the Validation Logjam

The single highest-leverage action is **batch dedup + batch validate**:

1. Dedup the queue (30 min) → reduces 27 to ~10–12
2. Batch quick-screen survivors (30 min each, ~6 hours total) → kill anything with single-fold OOS PF < 1.2
3. Full validate only the 3–5 that survive → ~12–20 hours total

This converts "108 hours of validation work" (27 × 4h) into ~20–30 hours spread over 2 weeks.
